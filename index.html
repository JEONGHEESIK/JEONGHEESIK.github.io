On-device AI research on preserving minority-critical signals under resource-constrained real-time environments.

On-Device AI Randomized SVD Isaac Sim Edge Inference
Ongoing Thesis Research
P: Conventional PCA/SVD-based compression tends to preserve dominant variance, which may cause rare but safety-critical signals to be diluted in low-dimensional representations under real-time on-device settings.
A: Designing a Task-Aware Randomized SVD architecture that incorporates task-sensitive weighting into randomized low-rank projection. The study is being structured around Isaac Sim-based scenarios with corner cases such as sudden obstacles and unstable control conditions.
R: Currently in progress. The goal is to verify whether minority-critical signal preservation can be improved while maintaining low latency and manageable memory usage in resource-constrained on-device environments.
